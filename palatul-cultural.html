The Cultural Palace ”Palatul Cultural“ is an early 20th century diverse multi–architectural style palace located in the city of Arad, Romania. The architecture of the building contains the Classic Italian Renaissance, Romanian Baroque, and French Gothic, elements and designs. The inspiration for some of the palace’s design came from the 15th century Corvin Castle, of Hunedoara, Romania.

It is a multi-purpose building that containes a library, a museum, an art gallery, and a concert hall. Arad was one of the busiest and most prosperous cities in Romania. They felt that as Arad grew and expanded, it needed to have its rich history and culture preserved.

CONCEPT OF THE NEW SYSTEM
The idea includes the incorporation of traditional embroideries.
Romania is a country characterised by its patriarchal values, which attaches great importance to the preservation of traditional structures. Apart from the most important symbol of tradition and national identity – the Othodox Church;
Rural traditionalism also plays a major role. Linen and wool were the most important materials for clothing, which was adorned with very decorative embroidery. Depending on the region, the patterns and colours of the embroideries were different. Thus, Arad also has its very own patterns and colours.
